DASHBOARD GUIDE

Healthcare Ransomware 2024 | 5,000 simulated incidents across hospitals, clinics, pharmacies, research labs, and insurance organizations. All three views are bidirectionally linked: actions in any view update the others.

VIEW 1: ECG MONITOR

A cardiac-monitor metaphor showing the cybersecurity health of each month in 2024. Each heartbeat peak represents one month. The waveform shape encodes severity: healthy months produce a clean heartbeat, while severely impacted months degrade into arrhythmia and ultimately flatline.

Normal Sinus Rhythm

SEVERITY 0 to 40. Clean, regular P-QRS-T complex.
Low infection rates, fast recovery, backups intact. The sector is healthy.

Arrhythmia

SEVERITY 40 to 65. Irregular rhythm, variable peaks.
Rising infections, slower recovery, some backups compromised. The sector is under stress.

Cardiac Arrest / Flatline

SEVERITY 65 to 100. Chaotic fibrillation to flatline.
Severe infections, weeks of downtime, data unrecoverable. The data has flatlined.

Severity Score (colored numbers above each month)

Each incident is scored using a weighted composite of four factors:

Infection Rate (30% weight): percentage of the organization's systems infected by ransomware, normalized against 90%.

Recovery Time (25% weight): days to restore operations, normalized against 120 days.

Data Loss (25% weight): inverse of data restored percentage. More data lost means higher severity.

Backup Compromise (20% weight): whether backups were destroyed. If yes, the full 20% is added.

Monthly severity is the average composite across all incidents in that month, then normalized to 0-100 against the selected organization type(s). The number's color matches the waveform: green for low severity, yellow for moderate, red for critical. Months with no incidents show a dashed line and "--".

Incident Count (n= below each month)

Shows how many ransomware incidents occurred in that month for the current selection. Updates as you filter by organization type, Sankey path, or time range.

Organization Type Filter (buttons in the View 1 header)

Filter all three views by the type of healthcare organization affected:

Hospital : large healthcare facilities with extensive patient data and critical care systems.

Clinic : smaller outpatient facilities, often with less IT infrastructure.

Pharma : pharmaceutical companies with research data and supply chain systems.

Lab : medical research institutions with sensitive experimental data.

Insurance : health insurance companies with extensive patient records and financial data.

Click multiple buttons to combine types. Click "All" or the ↻ reset icon to clear the filter.

Severity Gradient Bar (below the ECG)

A color reference showing the severity scale: green (low) through yellow (moderate) to red (critical). These colors match the waveform traces and the severity score numbers above each month.

Interactions

Brush Drag horizontally across months to select a time range. Views 2 and 3 update to show only incidents from those months. The selection persists until you click to clear it.

Org Filter Click one or more organization type buttons to filter all views. Sankey path selections are preserved when changing org type.

VIEW 2: ATTACK PATHOLOGY

Traces how each ransomware incident flows through six stages from initial entry to recovery outcome. Band thickness shows the proportional volume of incidents taking each path. Thicker bands mean more incidents followed that route.

Stage 1: Entry Vector

How the ransomware initially gained access to the organization:

Compromised Credentials : attackers obtained valid login credentials through stolen passwords, credential stuffing, or dark web purchases.

Exploited Vulnerability : attackers exploited an unpatched software vulnerability, including outdated systems or zero-day exploits.

Phishing Email : a malicious email tricked a user into clicking a link or opening an infected attachment.

RDP Exploit : attackers gained access through exposed Remote Desktop Protocol services, common in healthcare for remote access.

Malicious Website : drive-by download or watering hole attack through a compromised or malicious website.

Infected Removable Media : USB drives or other physical media carrying malware, introduced into the network.

Stage 2: Data Encrypted

Whether the ransomware successfully encrypted the organization's data, rendering it inaccessible until a decryption key is provided (usually for payment).

Yes = data was encrypted. No = encryption was prevented or failed.

Stage 3: Data Stolen

Whether attackers exfiltrated (stole) copies of the data before or during encryption. This is a double threat: even if data is recovered, stolen patient records can trigger privacy violations and regulatory consequences.

Stage 4: Backup Lost

Whether the organization's backup systems were also compromised or destroyed. Backup loss is one of the strongest predictors of extended recovery time and ransom payment, because the organization has no fallback to restore from.

Stage 5: Paid Ransom

Whether the organization paid the ransom demand. Payment does not guarantee full data recovery and may fund further attacks against other organizations.

Stage 6: Recovery Outcome

The percentage of data the organization ultimately recovered, regardless of method:

< 25% catastrophic loss   25-50% severe loss   50-75% partial recovery   75-100% good recovery

Visual Encoding

When nodes are selected, the diagram updates visually: matching bands turn neon-colored and adjust thickness to show filtered proportions, while non-matching bands dim to near-invisible. Nodes with zero matching incidents also dim, making it immediately clear which paths are active in your selection.

Interactions

Click Node Select one or more nodes per column to build a path filter. Selections within the same column combine with OR logic (e.g., "Phishing OR RDP"), while selections across columns combine with AND logic (e.g., "Phishing AND Encrypted: Yes"). The ECG recomputes and View 3 updates for matching incidents only.

Hover Node Shows a tooltip with the incident count, percentage of the current population, organization type, and time range. If clicking a node would produce zero results with the current selection, a red warning appears instead.

Hover Band Highlights the source and target column backgrounds with a blue glow, and the column titles turn green.

↻ Reset Clears all node selections and restores the default unfiltered view.

VIEW 3: RECOVERY ANALYSIS

A scatter plot exploring what organizational factors correlate with faster or slower recovery from ransomware attacks. Each dot is one incident. The X-axis is fixed to Recovery Time (Days) since recovery speed is the ultimate outcome of interest: dots on the left recovered quickly, dots on the right took longer.

"Measure" Dropdown (Y-axis)

Choose what to plot against recovery time:

Infection Rate : percentage of the organization's systems that were infected. Higher values indicate more widespread compromise. Helps answer: "Do more infected organizations take longer to recover?"

Data Recovery Rate : percentage of data ultimately recovered. Helps answer: "Do organizations with faster recovery also recover more data?"

Facilities Impacted : number of physical facilities (buildings, campuses) affected. Helps answer: "Does geographic spread affect recovery time?"

"Compare" Dropdown (dot color)

Change what the dot colors represent to visually compare groups:

Overall Severity : the composite severity score. A green-to-yellow-to-red gradient:

Low severity Moderate Critical

Monitoring Frequency : how often the organization actively monitors for cyber threats:

More than 1x/day
Daily
Weekly
Monthly

Threats Tracked : how many distinct cyber threat types the organization actively monitors:

1-50 threats
50-350 threats
350+ threats

Key insight: switching to "Monitoring Frequency" reveals that daily and sub-daily monitored organizations (green/cyan) cluster on the left (faster recovery), while monthly-monitored organizations (orange) spread to the right (slower recovery).

Drill-Down Detail Panel

Click any dot to see the full incident record in a panel: organization type and size, entry method, infection rate, recovery time, data restored percentage, facilities affected, and all binary outcomes (encrypted, stolen, backup lost, ransom paid). Also shows monitoring frequency and threats tracked. Hovering over a previously selected dot also displays its details.

Interactions

Click Dot Toggle individual incidents into your selection. Selected dots get a white border and enlarge. The ECG (View 1) recomputes severity for just those incidents, and the Sankey (View 2) adjusts band thickness to show their attack pathways.

Brush Drag a rectangle to add all enclosed dots to your selection. The brush rectangle clears after release so you can continue clicking individual dots or brush again to add more.

↻ Reset Clears all dot selections and restores Views 1 and 2 to their pre-selection state.

HOW THE VIEWS CONNECT

All three views are bidirectionally linked. Every action in one view propagates to the others:

View 1 → Views 2 and 3 : brushing months in the ECG filters the Sankey diagram and scatter plot to show only incidents from those months.

View 2 → Views 1 and 3 : clicking Sankey nodes recomputes the ECG waveforms for matching incidents and filters the scatter plot to show only those dots.

View 3 → Views 1 and 2 : selecting dots in the scatter plot recomputes ECG severity for those specific incidents and adjusts Sankey band thickness to show their attack pathways.

Org Filter → All Views : selecting organization types in View 1's header filters all three views. Sankey path selections persist across org type changes.

The three views tell a connected story: View 1 reveals WHEN attacks are most severe, View 2 shows HOW they unfold, and View 3 explores WHAT helps organizations recover faster.
